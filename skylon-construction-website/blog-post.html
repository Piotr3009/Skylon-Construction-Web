REFURBISHMENT·LONDON

Restoring character without faking it

Before any work begins, we establish what is original, what has already been altered and what is genuinely beyond repair. That assessment determines where to conserve, where to strengthen and where replacement is the more honest choice.

A successful repair should feel inevitable, not newly invented.

Sometimes replacement cannot be avoided. The challenge is then to reproduce the logic of the original rather than create an artificial copy of its age.

Profiles, proportions and construction methods are studied closely, but new work is not distressed simply to make it appear older. Over time, well-made joinery will settle naturally into its surroundings.

The best compliment a staircase can receive is that nobody notices it working.

Make the new work belong

The final finish should protect the work without hiding its history. Colour, sheen and surface preparation are chosen to bring old and new together while allowing both to remain truthful.

Honest restoration is a balance of judgement and restraint. Keep what still carries value, renew only what has failed, and make every new intervention worthy of what it joins.
