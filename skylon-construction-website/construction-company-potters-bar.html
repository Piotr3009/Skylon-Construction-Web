Potters Bar

Construction company
in Potters Bar.

Potters Bar sits inside Hertsmere, one of the most heavily Green Belt constrained boroughs in the country. Building well here starts with understanding what you are allowed to build.

Why Skylon

The cheapest mistake in construction is the one you catch before you build.

Potters Bar covers Oakmere, Parkfield, Furzefield, Bentley Heath and the surrounding villages. All of it sits under Hertsmere, and all of it is shaped by the Green Belt boundary.

Hertsmere Borough Council is the planning authority. We price and programme against the authority that will actually decide your application, and we say what is not possible before you have paid for a design that cannot be built.

Building in Potters Bar

Green Belt on almost every side

  • Around 80 percent of Hertsmere is Green Belt. Potters Bar is physically separate from the rest of the borough and hemmed in by protected land. Extensions and replacement dwellings near the boundary are tested on volume and openness before design is even considered.
  • Inter-war and Edwardian housing stock. Much of the town is solid, well built and worth keeping. We work with the existing structure rather than fighting it, which usually costs less and always looks better.
  • Plot depth is the opportunity. Rear extensions and basements often deliver more usable space than a side return. We test the options against real cost before you commit to a design.
  • Our office is 20 minutes away. St Albans to Potters Bar is a short run, which means our site manager is present, not calling in.
