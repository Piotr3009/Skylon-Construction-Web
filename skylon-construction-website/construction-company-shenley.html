Construction company
in Shenley.

Shenley is a Green Belt village that became more suburban after the former hospital site was redeveloped. That history shapes what can and cannot be built here.

Why Skylon

The cheapest mistake in construction is the one you catch before you build.

Shenley sits under Hertsmere, the same authority as Radlett, Borehamwood, Bushey and Potters Bar. We know how this council reads an application.

Hertsmere Borough Council is the planning authority. We price and programme against the authority that will actually decide your application, and we say what is not possible before you have paid for a design that cannot be built.

Building in Shenley

A Green Belt village, not a suburb

  • Green Belt policy applies to most of the surrounding land. Hertsmere is roughly 80 percent Green Belt. In a village like Shenley, that shapes every application on the edge of the built-up area.
  • Mixed stock, mixed rules. Original village properties and later hospital site housing sit side by side. What is permitted on one street is not automatically permitted on the next.
  • Character matters here. Applications are judged against village character, not just policy. Materials, roof form and massing carry real weight.
  • Access is genuinely tight. Village lanes are not built for construction traffic. Deliveries and plant need planning before mobilisation.
