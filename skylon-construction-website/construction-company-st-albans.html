Building in St Albans

A Roman city with modern rules

  • Conservation areas and listed buildings. The historic core carries a dense concentration of protected fabric. Window profiles, mortar mixes, roof coverings and rainwater goods are all scrutinised. We specify to survive the conservation officer, not just the drawing.
  • Green Belt on the edges. Plots outside the built-up area fall under Green Belt policy, where extensions and replacement dwellings are tested on volume and openness before anything else.
  • Archaeology. Verulamium means groundworks in and around the city can trigger archaeological conditions. That is a programme risk we plan for, not a surprise we discover.
  • Tight streets, real neighbours. Deliveries, scaffold licences and working hours need agreeing up front. Our site managers handle this before the first lorry arrives.
